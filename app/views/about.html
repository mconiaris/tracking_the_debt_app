So, it was a really easy decision to make this site. It meets a long standing need that the Internet, for some reason, has ignored.

So seriously, why did you make this page?

This is my final project for the August Web Development Immersive of General Assembly, a software development school, where I learned all sorts of things, including Ruby, Ruby on Rails, Postgres, Redis, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery and more importantly, how do learn new stuff on my own.

This site is an exmaple of learning on my own, as I had no prior experience using node.js, Grunt or the Karma testing suite. It was a lot of trial and error to make it work.

I enjoy working with data, and wanted to explore data visualization a little bit more, as well as get more reps with Ruby and Rails and JavaScript. This is a humble (and pretty quick) attempt at doing that.

What is it that you are not trying to do?

I am not making an editorial on the national debt, why it exists and whose fault it is. I certainly have my opinions, which I will be happy to share with you after I get a real job. In the meantime, feel free to use this site as you wish and sleep better knowing that you can find this kind of data on the Internet free of charge.

How did you build this site?

This site was built using AngularJS, Bootstrap, Dygraphs, Grunt, JavaScript and a little bit of jQuery thrown in. Learning Dygraphs with Angular was the biggest challenge and sometimes Grunt was difficult. Stack Overflow was my friend!

How can I use this site?

Choose which one of the six charts that you'd like to look at and feel free to select more detailed data by using your mouse. It's that simple!
